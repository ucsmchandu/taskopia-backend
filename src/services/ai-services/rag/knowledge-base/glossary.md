# Taskopia Glossary

## Overview

This glossary explains common terms used throughout the Taskopia platform.

Understanding these terms helps users navigate the platform and understand how Taskopia works.

---

# A

## Accepted Application

An application that has been selected by a Host.

Once an application is accepted, the Ally becomes responsible for completing the task and chat becomes available.

---

## Ally

An Ally is a registered Taskopia user who applies for and completes tasks posted by Hosts.

Allies can browse tasks, submit applications, communicate after acceptance, complete work, and receive ratings.

---

## Application

An application is a request submitted by an Ally expressing interest in completing a task.

Submitting an application does not guarantee selection.

---

## AI Help Assistant

The AI Help Assistant is Taskopia's chatbot that answers platform-related questions using the Taskopia knowledge base.

It uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to provide accurate answers.

---

## AI Translation

An AI-powered feature that translates task descriptions and chat messages between different languages to improve communication.

---

# B

## Budget

The amount of money a Host offers for completing a task.

The budget is displayed before Allies apply.

---

# C

## Category

A classification used to organize tasks.

Examples include:

- Home Services
- Delivery
- Technical
- Education
- Events

Choosing the correct category improves task discovery.

---

## Chat

Taskopia's in-platform messaging system.

Chat becomes available only after a Host accepts an Ally's application.

It is intended only for task-related communication.

---

## Completed Task

A task that has been successfully finished and confirmed by the Host.

Completed tasks become part of the user's history.

---

# D

## Dashboard

A personalized area where users manage their activities.

Hosts manage tasks and applications.

Allies manage applications, accepted tasks, and completed work.

---

# E

## Estimated Duration

The approximate amount of time required to complete a task.

This information helps Allies decide whether they are available.

---

# F

## Feedback

Comments or suggestions submitted by users to improve the platform.

Feedback may also refer to written reviews left after task completion.

---

# H

## Help Center

A collection of guides, documentation, and frequently asked questions designed to assist users.

---

## Host

A Host is a registered Taskopia user who creates and manages tasks.

Hosts review applications, select Allies, and oversee task completion.

---

# K

## Knowledge Base

A collection of Taskopia documentation used by the AI Help Assistant.

The RAG chatbot retrieves information from this knowledge base before answering user questions.

---

# L

## Location

The place where a task will be completed.

Hosts should provide accurate location information.

---

# M

## Marketplace

The section of Taskopia where published tasks are displayed for Allies to discover and apply.

---

## Moderation

The process of reviewing reports, investigating policy violations, and taking appropriate action to maintain a safe community.

---

# N

## Notification

An alert informing users about important events such as:

- New applications
- Accepted applications
- Chat messages
- Task updates

---

# P

## Payment

The agreed amount paid by the Host after successful task completion.

Payment arrangements are made between the Host and the Ally.

---

## Platform

Taskopia itself.

The platform provides tools for posting tasks, discovering work, communication, reporting, and AI assistance.

Taskopia does not perform or supervise offline work.

---

## Profile

A user's personal page containing information such as:

- Name
- Bio
- Skills
- Ratings
- Reviews
- Completed tasks

---

# R

## RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

A technique used by the AI Help Assistant where relevant Taskopia documentation is retrieved before generating an answer.

This improves answer accuracy.

---

## Rating

A score given after task completion.

Ratings help build user reputation.

---

## Review

Written feedback describing a user's experience after completing a task.

Reviews help other users make informed decisions.

---

## Report

A complaint submitted through the Report Problem feature regarding abuse, fraud, fake tasks, or other policy violations.

---

# S

## Search

The feature that allows Allies to find tasks using keywords, categories, locations, and filters.

---

## Status

The current stage of a task.

Common statuses include:

- Draft
- Open
- Assigned
- In Progress
- Completed
- Cancelled
- Closed

---

## Support

The Taskopia team that assists users with account issues, technical problems, feedback, and general inquiries.

---

# T

## Task

A short-term job created by a Host.

Tasks contain information such as title, description, location, payment, category, and schedule.

---

## Task Description

A detailed explanation of the work to be completed.

Clear descriptions help Allies understand expectations before applying.

---

## Task ID

A unique identifier assigned to every task.

Task IDs help identify tasks when contacting support or reporting problems.

---

## Task Lifecycle

The complete journey of a task from creation to completion.

Typical stages include:

- Creation
- Publication
- Applications
- Selection
- Chat
- Completion
- Ratings

---

## Task Poster

Another name for a Host.

A Task Poster creates and manages tasks.

---

# U

## User

Any registered member of Taskopia.

Users may register as either a Host or an Ally.

---

# V

## Verification

The process of confirming user or account information to improve trust and security.

---

# W

## Work History

A record of completed tasks associated with a user's account.

Work history contributes to reputation.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## What is the difference between a Host and a Task Poster?

There is no difference.

Both terms refer to the user who creates and manages tasks.

---

## What is an Ally?

An Ally is the person who applies for and completes tasks.

---

## What is RAG?

RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

It allows the AI Help Assistant to search Taskopia's knowledge base before generating answers.

---

## What is a completed task?

A completed task is one that has been finished by the Ally and confirmed by the Host.

---

## What is a report?

A report is a complaint submitted to Taskopia regarding policy violations, fraud, abuse, or other inappropriate behaviour.

---

# Summary

The Taskopia Glossary provides definitions for the most important platform terms.

It serves as a quick reference for users and supports the AI Help Assistant by providing clear, consistent explanations of Taskopia-specific concepts.
